The Iteration Automater must enforce:

1. readiness assessment before execution or scale;
2. weakest-essential-barrier selection;
3. approved gap-response routing;
4. maximum-safe-commitment limits;
5. one active objective, decision, question, reversible action, and evidence review when complexity blocks progress;
6. movement detection: every cycle must produce a decision, action, experiment, contact, validated artifact, external result, or evidence entry;
7. hold, stop, rollback, defer, fork, or reject behavior when readiness or evidence is insufficient;
8. preservation of prior decisions and evidence when a route changes;
9. compliance records written to the APEX Evidence Ledger.
